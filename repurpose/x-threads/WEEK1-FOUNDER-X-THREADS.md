# Repurposed X Threads (from Founder LinkedIn)

## Thread 1 — Guest chaos is mostly systems, not romance
1/ Most destination wedding stress doesn’t come from venues. It comes from guest coordination.
2/ Building Addressed, we keep seeing this: couples don’t need more planning advice—they need fewer places for critical guest info to get lost.
3/ If communication, RSVP logic, and guest details live in one workflow, confidence rises fast.
4/ The goal isn’t “more features.” It’s operational calm before travel.
5/ If you build in events/weddings: where does avoidable chaos show up most?

## Thread 2 — Premium is an operations decision
1/ Founder lesson: “Beautiful” and “useful” can’t be separate teams in wedding tech.
2/ Destination weddings must feel premium AND run reliably.
3/ If RSVP workflows break on plus-ones/sub-events/dietary notes, trust drops—fast.
4/ We’re focused on: reduce manual follow-up, centralize communication, keep guest touchpoints clear.
5/ Premium experience is an operations decision. Agree or disagree?

## Thread 3 — Channel mismatch is hidden friction
1/ I used to think planning friction was mostly “organizational.”
2/ Now I think it’s mostly “channel mismatch.”
3/ Guests live in SMS, WhatsApp, email, and calls. Many tools assume one channel + perfect behavior.
4/ That gap creates follow-up fatigue.
5/ Meet guests where they are, but keep one source of truth for planners/couples.
6/ Are your comms designed around your system—or user behavior?
