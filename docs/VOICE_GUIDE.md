# Voice Guide: Addressed

## Brand Positioning (working)
Addressed is the modern guest management platform for destination weddings — built for couples and planners who want the romance of travel with the confidence of flawless coordination.

## Core Voice
- **Warm:** human, emotionally intelligent, never robotic
- **Modern:** clean language, no old-school wedding clichés
- **Travel-romantic:** inspired by premium travel brands (e.g., Fora-style tone)
- **Operationally confident:** practical, precise, and in control

## Tone Formula
**Romance + Logistics + Momentum**

Write copy that inspires first, then immediately reduces planning friction.

## Audience Tone Split

### For DIY couples
- Supportive and empowering
- “You’ve got this, and we’ll make it easier”
- Emphasize simplicity, confidence, and guest experience

### For planners
- Professional and efficiency-forward
- Emphasize workflow reliability, time savings, and client polish

## Brand Language

### We say
- destination wedding experience
- guest communication made simple
- RSVP clarity
- travel-ready guest management
- smooth planning flow
- less chasing, more celebrating

### We avoid
- “bridezilla,” “perfect day” clichés, overly saccharine language
- vague AI hype without utility
- stiff corporate jargon

## Writing Rules
1. Lead with a vivid emotional hook tied to travel/experience
2. Follow with concrete utility (what problem gets solved)
3. Keep sentences clean and skimmable
4. End with one clear CTA
5. Sound premium but accessible

## CTA Style
- Primary: soft-confident invitation
  - “Start your guest list the modern way.”
  - “Make destination RSVP tracking effortless.”
- Secondary: planner-oriented outcomes
  - “Deliver a smoother guest experience at scale.”

## Content Pillars
1. Destination wedding planning tips
2. Guest list + RSVP systems
3. Communication workflows (SMS/WhatsApp/email)
4. Real planning scenarios and fixes
5. Modern etiquette + digital invitation strategy

## Compliance / Safety Notes
- No unverifiable performance claims
- No guarantees of outcomes
- Testimonials/case studies should be flagged for review before publishing

## Draft Tagline Directions
- "Destination weddings, beautifully coordinated."
- "For couples whose love language is let’s book a trip."
- "Romantic plans. Reliable guest management."
